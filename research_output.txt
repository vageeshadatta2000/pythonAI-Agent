The whale shark (Rhincodon typus) and the basking shark (Cetorhinus maximus), both of which may weigh several tons, are harmless giants that subsist on plankton strained from the sea through modified gill rakers.
Whale sharks may grow up to 18 metres (59 feet) in length, whereas basking sharks may reach 14 metres (46 feet) fully grown.

Sharks are some of the most fascinating and diverse creatures in the ocean, with over 500 species identified worldwide.
Among these, there are many types of sharks that vary greatly in size, shape, and behavior.
From the massive Whale Shark to the sleek and speedy Great White Shark, the diversity of these apex predators is truly remarkable.
